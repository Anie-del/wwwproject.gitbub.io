Origin and History of Pastries

Explore the rich history of pastries, from their humble beginnings to modern favorites...

Koeksisters

    Koeksisters have roots in Cape Malay cuisine, blending Dutch and Malaysian influences. The name "koeksister" is derived from the Dutch word "koek," meaning cake. These pastries have become a symbol of South African culinary diversity.

Anzac Biscuits

    Anzac Biscuits were sent by wives to soldiers during WWI due to their long shelf life. The name "Anzac" (Australian and New Zealand Army Corps) pays homage to the troops.

Empanadas

    Empanadas have a rich history dating back to medieval Spain and were later brought to Latin America by Spanish explorers. Each region has added its unique twist, making it a versatile and beloved dish.

Croissant

    The croissant has Viennese origins but gained fame in France. Legend has it that the croissant was inspired by the crescent moon on the Ottoman flag during the 17th-century Battle of Vienna.

Samosa

    Samosas have a rich history in Indian and Middle Eastern cuisines, believed to have been introduced by traders along ancient trade routes. They have evolved into a beloved snack enjoyed worldwide.

Bannock

    Bannock has deep roots in Indigenous cultures across North America. It was introduced by European settlers and adapted into various Indigenous cuisines, each with its unique variations.

Baklava

    Baklava's origins trace back to the Ottoman Empire, evolving over centuries across the Middle East, Balkans, and Mediterranean. Each culture has added its unique touch, creating a rich and diverse history.

Tiramisu

    Tiramisu, meaning "pick me up" in Italian, has roots in the Veneto region. Its origin is debated, but it gained popularity in the 1960s, becoming a staple in Italian and international dessert menus.
